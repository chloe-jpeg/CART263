Project one Response - Chloé Guérin

Can You See Me by Yelena Arakelian and Scarlett Yvonne Arriola Lerin

Can You See Me is a 2 player game where one of the players is visible at all time and has to look for the other, who appears rarely and moves around without warning. The game is played using arrow keys and WASD for each player, and you have a certain amount of time for the players to find each other before they lose. 

What I really liked about their work is how clean the game looks. There is clearly a theme that's a bit cryptic and scary, reminiscent of a Victorian gothic aesthetic and it’s well assumed throughout the opening screen and the game. There are 2 screens, the opening with clear explanations and the actual gameplay, and they both look linked to each other. Even the endings, whether you win or lose, fits in well with the rest of the game. The music also adds a nice touch to the whole experience. The game, while simple, works well and I did not notice any issues, glitches or bugs. The controls are smooth and well executed and everything looks clean. I went a bit too fast the first time I played so I missed the instructions, but even without it was easy to figure out and win. The use of the GIFTs added a nice little animation. 

In terms of the code, the whole file is very clean and organized. I was surprised by the size of the html file, I expected something a bit bigger but after thinking about it it made sense since the game is quite simple and there are only 2 “pages” to it, start screen and actual gameplay. The css is a lot longer in comparison but still clean and well defined, sections are organised in a way that makes it easy for people to find something specific. As for the javascript, the same can be said for the file, everything is nicely organized and easy to follow along. I liked that there were clearly some thoughts put into little details, like the whole section about making the angels did not overlap and would push each other away, even going as far as making sure the bounce wasn’t set too high to keep with the floaty feeling of the game. While playing the game I did not notice that the player 2 was moving at set intervals, I had to see that in the code. I really liked that they added the movement only after player one starts moving, just to make the game a bit more challenging. 

Overall, the game, while simple, is nice and well made,the graphics are interesting and the code is clean and easy to follow along with which leaves a nice finish.
